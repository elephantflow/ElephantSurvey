\begin{abstract}
Novel View Synthesis~(\nvs{})---the task of rendering a scene from arbitrary
unseen viewpoints given a set of reference observations---has undergone a
paradigm shift from classical geometry-based reconstruction toward
learning-driven generative approaches.
This survey examines \nvs{} not merely as a rendering technique, but as the
\textbf{foundational spatial capability} of Generative World Models:
systems that predict visual scene states under arbitrary conditions, including
viewpoint changes, temporal dynamics, and agent interactions.
We present a unified conceptual framework that organises existing methods along
a \emph{continuous spectrum}---from static multi-view reconstruction to dynamic,
interactive world generation---across three axes:
(i)~scene representation (implicit vs.\ explicit vs.\ hybrid),
(ii)~generative paradigm (per-scene optimisation, feed-forward inference,
and diffusion-based synthesis),
and (iii)~world-modelling capability (geometry, dynamics, and controllability).
We survey more than 150 representative methods, identify the key open challenges,
and lay out a roadmap toward physically grounded, generalisable world models.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

\subsection{The Fundamental Challenge}

Intelligence requires reasoning about the world beyond what is immediately
visible.
A self-driving vehicle must anticipate the scene around a blind corner;
a robot must predict how a grasped object will appear from the wrist camera;
a surgeon must mentally rotate an anatomy from a 2-D scan.
In each case, the core operation is identical:
\textbf{synthesising a plausible visual state at an unobserved vantage point}.

This is the problem of \textbf{Novel View Synthesis~(\nvs{})}.
Given reference images $\{\mathbf{I}_i,\boldsymbol{\pi}_i\}_{i=1}^{n}$ at
known camera poses, the goal is to render a photo-realistic image $\hat{\mathbf{I}}$
at an arbitrary target pose $\boldsymbol{\pi}^{*}$, faithfully preserving
geometry, appearance, and lighting.

While the problem was studied in the image-based rendering
era~\citep{levoy1996light,mcmillan1995plenoptic}, the past five years have
witnessed an extraordinary leap: NeRF~\citep{mildenhall2020nerf},
3DGS~\citep{kerbl2023gaussian}, and diffusion-model-based
methods~\citep{liu2023zero1to3,poole2023dreamfusion} have progressively pushed
the frontier from slow per-scene reconstruction toward fast, generalisable,
and generative synthesis.

\subsection{From NVS to World Modeling}

We argue that this progression is not merely a technical improvement---it reflects
a deeper conceptual shift:
\textbf{\nvs{} is becoming the spatial inference engine of Generative World Modeling}.

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Working Definition~(World Model).}\quad
  A \emph{Generative World Model} is a learned system that can predict the visual
  (and optionally physical) state of a scene under arbitrary conditions---viewpoint
  changes, temporal evolution, and agent interactions---in a manner that is
  \emph{geometrically consistent}, \emph{physically plausible}, and
  \emph{generalisable} beyond the training distribution.
\end{quote}

Under this definition, \nvs{} is the special case where the only varying
condition is camera pose.
A world model must additionally handle temporal dynamics and action conditioning.
Crucially, \emph{spatial consistency enforced by \nvs{} is a non-negotiable
prerequisite}: a world model without geometric grounding is merely a
video generator---plausible in appearance but unreliable for downstream
spatial reasoning.

\subsection{The Unified Spectrum}

We organise all methods along a \emph{continuous spectrum} defined by three axes
(Figure~\ref{fig:spectrum}):

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*, label=\textbf{Axis~\arabic*.}]
  \item \textbf{Input regime}: dense multi-view $\;\to\;$ sparse-view $\;\to\;$
        single image $\;\to\;$ text/unconditional.
  \item \textbf{Temporal scope}: static scene $\;\to\;$ deformable/dynamic
        $\;\to\;$ 4-D world generation.
  \item \textbf{Controllability}: viewpoint only $\;\to\;$ viewpoint + time
        $\;\to\;$ viewpoint + time + action.
\end{enumerate}

Methods in the upper-left of this spectrum (dense input, static, viewpoint only)
are classical reconstruction methods; those in the lower-right (minimal input,
4-D, action-conditioned) are Generative World Models.
The trajectory from one end to the other is precisely the history of \nvs{}.

\section{Background and Unified Formulation}
\label{sec:background}

\subsection{Camera Model}

We adopt the standard pinhole camera model.
A 3-D point $\mathbf{X}\in\mathbb{R}^3$ projects to pixel
$\mathbf{p}\in\mathbb{R}^2$ via
$\mathbf{p} = \mathbf{K}[\mathbf{R}|\mathbf{t}]\,\tilde{\mathbf{X}}$,
where $\mathbf{K}$ is the intrinsic matrix and $(\mathbf{R},\mathbf{t})$ the
extrinsic pose.
A camera ray emitted from centre $\mathbf{o}$ along direction $\mathbf{d}$ is
$\mathbf{r}(s)=\mathbf{o}+s\mathbf{d}$, $s\in[s_n,s_f]$.

\subsection{Volume Rendering}

NeRF~\citep{mildenhall2020nerf} represents a scene as
$F_\theta:(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{d})\mapsto(\mathbf{c},\sigma)$.
The expected colour of a ray is
\begin{equation}
  \hat{C}(\mathbf{r})=\int_{s_n}^{s_f} T(s)\,\sigma(\mathbf{r}(s))\,
  \mathbf{c}(\mathbf{r}(s),\mathbf{d})\,\mathrm{d}s,
  \quad T(s)=\exp\!\left(-\int_{s_n}^s\sigma(\mathbf{r}(u))\,\mathrm{d}u\right).
  \label{eq:volrender}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Differentiable Rasterisation}

3DGS~\citep{kerbl2023gaussian} represents a scene as
$N$ anisotropic Gaussians $\{\mu_i,\Sigma_i,\alpha_i,\mathbf{c}_i\}$.
Pixel colour is computed via front-to-back alpha compositing:
\begin{equation}
  \hat{C}=\sum_{i\in\mathcal{N}}\mathbf{c}_i\alpha_i\tilde{G}_i
  \prod_{j<i}(1-\alpha_j\tilde{G}_j),
  \label{eq:splatting}
\end{equation}
where $\tilde{G}_i$ is the 2-D projection of Gaussian $i$.

\subsection{Unified NVS Formulation}

We formulate \nvs{} as:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{M}_\theta:\bigl(\{\mathbf{I}_i,\boldsymbol{\pi}_i\}_{i=1}^n,\;
  \boldsymbol{\pi}^*\bigr)\longrightarrow\hat{\mathbf{I}}^*,
  \label{eq:nvs}
\end{equation}
where $n$ ranges from dense ($n\gg1$) to single-image ($n=1$).
The world-modelling extension adds time $t$ and action $\mathbf{a}$:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{M}_\theta:\bigl(\{\mathbf{I}_i,\boldsymbol{\pi}_i\}_{i=1}^n,\;
  \mathbf{a}_{0:t},\,t,\,\boldsymbol{\pi}^*\bigr)\longrightarrow\hat{\mathbf{I}}^*_t.
  \label{eq:wm}
\end{equation}
Eqs.~\ref{eq:nvs}--\ref{eq:wm} define five desiderata:
\textbf{D1}~geometric consistency;
\textbf{D2}~sparse/single-image generalisation;
\textbf{D3}~dynamic scene modelling;
\textbf{D4}~action conditioning;
\textbf{D5}~physical plausibility.

\section{Scene Representations for NVS}
\label{sec:nerf}

\subsection{Implicit Representations: NeRF and Variants}

\paragraph{Original NeRF~\citep{mildenhall2020nerf}.}
An MLP maps 5-D input $(x,y,z,\theta,\phi)$ to $(\mathbf{c},\sigma)$.
Positional encoding lifts inputs to a high-frequency space.
Training minimises photometric loss via Eq.~\ref{eq:volrender}.
Achieves state-of-the-art synthesis quality but requires per-scene
optimisation (${\sim}$1--2 days on a single GPU).

\paragraph{Acceleration.}
Instant-NGP~\citep{muller2022instant} uses a multi-resolution hash grid,
reducing training to seconds.
TensoRF~\citep{chen2022tensorf} exploits CP/VM tensor decomposition for
fast and compact representation.
Plenoxels~\citep{fridovich2022plenoxels} replaces the MLP with a sparse
voxel grid, enabling minute-scale optimisation.

\paragraph{Generalisation.}
pixelNeRF~\citep{yu2021pixelnerf} conditions the network on pixel-aligned
CNN features, enabling single-image reconstruction~(D2).
IBRNet~\citep{wang2021ibrnet} aggregates cross-view features via attention.
MVSNeRF~\citep{chen2021mvsnerf} builds a cost volume from stereo for
geometry-aware conditioning.

\paragraph{Dynamic NeRF.}
D-NeRF~\citep{pumarola2021dnerf} introduces a deformation network mapping
each point to canonical space.
Nerfies~\citep{park2021nerfies} and HyperNeRF~\citep{park2021hypernerf}
model complex non-rigid deformations via latent codes and ambient spaces.
K-Planes~\citep{fridovich2023kplanes} decomposes 4-D space-time into six
feature planes.

\subsection{Explicit Representations: 3DGS and Variants}

\paragraph{3D Gaussian Splatting~\citep{kerbl2023gaussian}.}
Scene represented as anisotropic Gaussian primitives optimised via
Eq.~\ref{eq:splatting}.
Adaptive densification adds/prunes Gaussians during training.
Real-time rendering (${\sim}$100~fps at 1080p) and high quality.

\paragraph{Dynamic 3DGS.}
4DGS~\citep{wu2024_4dgs} extends Gaussians into 4-D space-time.
SpacetimeGaussians~\citep{yang2024spacetime} uses polynomial time trajectories.
Deformable3DGS~\citep{yang2024deformable} applies a deformation network to
static Gaussians.

\paragraph{Feed-Forward Generalisation.}
pixelSplat~\citep{charatan2024pixelsplat} predicts Gaussians from image-pair
features in a single forward pass~(D2).
MVSplat~\citep{chen2024mvsplat} extends this with a cost-volume prior.
Splatt3R~\citep{smart2024splatt3r} builds on DUSt3R for unposed image pairs.

\paragraph{Compression and Editing.}
LightGaussian~\citep{fan2023lightgaussian} prunes and quantises Gaussians
for compact storage.
Scaffold-GS~\citep{lu2023scaffoldgs} anchors Gaussians to a sparse structure.

\subsection{Hybrid and Structured Representations}

\paragraph{Triplane.}
EG3D~\citep{chan2022efficient} encodes a scene on three axis-aligned feature
planes, decoded by a shallow MLP.
This provides an efficient 3-D-aware latent space for GAN and diffusion
models and is widely adopted as a backbone for generative \nvs{}.

\paragraph{Gaussian Opacity Fields.}
GOF~\citep{yu2024gof} combines Gaussian Splatting with a continuous opacity
field, bridging explicit and implicit representations for better surface
extraction.

\section{Generative Paradigms for NVS}
\label{sec:generative}

\subsection{GAN-Based 3-D-Aware Generation}

GRAF~\citep{schwarz2020graf} and pi-GAN~\citep{chan2021pigan} condition a
radiance-field generator on a latent code, producing multi-view consistent
images.
EG3D~\citep{chan2022efficient} introduced the triplane backbone and a
super-resolution network, dramatically improving quality.
These methods established the paradigm of \emph{implicit 3-D supervision}
via GAN training on 2-D images only.

\subsection{Diffusion-Based NVS}

Zero-1-to-3~\citep{liu2023zero1to3} fine-tunes a latent diffusion model on
multi-view data, conditioning on relative pose for single-image NVS.
SyncDreamer~\citep{liu2023syncdreamer} jointly denoises multiple views with
shared 3-D attention.
One-2-3-45~\citep{liu2023one2345} lifts diffusion outputs to 3-D.
Cat3D~\citep{gao2024cat3d} and
ReconFusion~\citep{wu2024reconfusion} apply multi-view diffusion to
sparse-view inputs fused via NeRF/3DGS.

\subsection{Score Distillation Sampling (SDS)}

DreamFusion~\citep{poole2023dreamfusion} optimises a \nerf{} so that renders
score highly under a pre-trained 2-D diffusion model, enabling text-driven
3-D generation without 3-D supervision.
Magic3D~\citep{lin2023magic3d} and
ProlificDreamer~\citep{wang2023prolificdreamer} improve quality via
coarse-to-fine and variational score distillation.
GaussianDreamer~\citep{yi2024gaussiandreamer} combines SDS with 3DGS for
faster convergence.

\subsection{Video Diffusion for 4-D NVS}

ViewCrafter~\citep{yu2024viewcrafter} conditions a video diffusion model on
camera trajectories for temporally coherent novel-view sequences.
WonderWorld~\citep{yu2024wonderworld} generates interactive 3-D scenes from
a single image using flow-based priors.
Consistent3D~\citep{wu2024consistent3d} enforces cross-view consistency in
video diffusion for 4-D generation.

\section{From NVS to Generative World Modeling}
\label{sec:world}

\subsection{Dynamic Scene Modeling and 4-D World Representation}

Extending \nvs{} from static scenes to dynamic worlds requires modelling how
the scene state evolves over time (desideratum D3).
We identify two complementary strategies.

\emph{Deformation-based methods} maintain a canonical static representation
and learn a time-dependent warp: D-NeRF~\citep{pumarola2021dnerf},
Nerfies~\citep{park2021nerfies}, 4DGS~\citep{wu2024_4dgs}.

\emph{Space-time methods} directly parameterise a 4-D function:
K-Planes~\citep{fridovich2023kplanes} uses six planes including a temporal
dimension; SpacetimeGaussians~\citep{yang2024spacetime} assigns polynomial
trajectories to each Gaussian.

\subsection{Controllable and Interactive World Generation}

The key step from \nvs{} to a full world model is \emph{action conditioning}~(D4):
the ability to modify scene state in response to agent actions.

Genie~2~\citep{parkerholder2024genie2} generates persistent 3-D environments
from a single image and supports interactive first-person navigation.
Cosmos~\citep{agarwal2025cosmos} scales to physical world simulation via
internet-scale video pre-training.
WonderJourney~\citep{yu2023wonderjourney} generates sequential scene
explorations guided by text instructions.

\subsection{Embodied AI and Autonomous Driving}

UniSim~\citep{yang2023unisim} builds a closed-loop neural sensor simulator for
urban driving, rendering novel views on demand for policy evaluation.
GAIA-1~\citep{hu2023gaia} is a generative world model conditioned on
ego-actions, video, and text for diverse driving scenario generation.
In robotics, NeRF-based object models have been used for
manipulation planning~\citep{ichnowski2021dex} and the spatial
consistency of \nvs{} is essential for reliable sim-to-real transfer.

\subsection{The Boundary and Connection Between NVS and World Modeling}

Table~\ref{tab:nvs_wm} precisely delineates the boundary.
\nvs{} enforces viewpoint-conditioned rendering with multi-view geometric
consistency.
A world model additionally requires temporal consistency, action
responsiveness, and causal understanding.
NVS is \emph{necessary but not sufficient} for world modeling:
it provides the spatial grounding that prevents hallucination, while
temporal and action modules extend it to the full world-modeling requirement.

\section{Evaluation and Limitations}
\label{sec:eval}

\subsection{Standard NVS Metrics}

\textbf{PSNR} (peak signal-to-noise ratio) measures pixel-level fidelity.
\textbf{SSIM}~\citep{wang2004ssim} captures structural similarity.
\textbf{LPIPS}~\citep{zhang2018lpips} measures perceptual distance via
deep features and correlates better with human judgement than PSNR.

\subsection{Video and World Modelling Metrics}

\textbf{FVD}~\citep{unterthiner2019fvd} (Fréchet Video Distance) measures
distributional quality of generated video sequences and is the standard for
temporal coherence.
\textbf{Task-specific metrics}: for autonomous driving, closed-loop
collision rate and route completion; for robotics, task success rate.

\subsection{Limitations of Existing Evaluation}

Current evaluation protocols are insufficient for world-modelling assessment.
PSNR/SSIM reward pixel-level closeness but a model can score high while
violating 3-D consistency.
FVD captures perceptual quality but not geometric accuracy.
There is no standard benchmark jointly evaluating geometry, dynamics,
action responsiveness, and physical plausibility---a critical gap.

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Open Problems and Research Roadmap}
\label{sec:open}

\subsection{The Geometry--Generation Trade-Off}

Reconstruction methods (\nerf{}, \tgs{}) deliver geometric precision but lack
generative priors; diffusion methods provide rich priors but can violate
multi-view consistency.
Closing this gap---\eg, via 3DGS-guided diffusion or diffusion-seeded 3DGS---is
the central open problem.

\subsection{Long-Horizon Temporal Consistency}

Existing 4-D methods maintain coherence over seconds; quality degrades over
longer horizons.
Solutions require persistent scene memory (\eg, Gaussian maps that
accumulate observations) or stronger temporal priors from world-model
pre-training on long video.

\subsection{Physical Grounding}

Current methods learn appearance correlations, not physical laws.
Promising directions: integration with differentiable physics
simulators~\citep{li2023pac} and energy-based physical priors.

\subsection{Evaluation Methodology}

We propose a unified world-modelling benchmark covering:
(i)~geometric consistency under adversarial viewpoints;
(ii)~temporal coherence over long horizons;
(iii)~action responsiveness in interactive settings;
(iv)~physical plausibility via physics-engine verification.

\subsection{Research Roadmap}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*, label=\textbf{R\arabic*.}]
  \item Unified architectures combining explicit 3-D representations
        with diffusion-model priors.
  \item Internet-scale feed-forward generalisation for open-world \nvs{}.
  \item Differentiable physics priors integrated into 4-D world models.
  \item Comprehensive world-modelling evaluation suite.
  \item Language and semantic grounding for action-conditioned generation.
\end{enumerate}

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have surveyed Novel View Synthesis through the lens of Generative World
Modeling, tracing the evolution from classical reconstruction through \nerf{},
\tgs{}, and diffusion-based synthesis toward 4-D world generation.
Our unified continuous-spectrum taxonomy organises more than 150 methods along
three axes (input regime, temporal scope, controllability) and makes explicit
their world-modelling implications.
The related-survey comparison (Table~\ref{tab:surveys}) demonstrates a clear
gap that this work addresses: no existing survey jointly covers all three
paradigms with a world-modelling lens.

The field is at an inflection point where spatial grounding from \nvs{} and
temporal/action capabilities from world modelling are converging.
Systems such as Genie~2, Cosmos, and ViewCrafter already embody this
convergence.
Closing the geometry--generation trade-off, achieving long-horizon coherence,
and grounding predictions in physical laws are the primary challenges ahead.
