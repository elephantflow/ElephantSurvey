\begin{abstract}
Novel View Synthesis~(\nvs)---rendering a scene from arbitrary unseen viewpoints
given reference observations---has undergone a paradigm shift from classical
geometry-based reconstruction toward learning-driven generative approaches.
This survey examines \nvs{} not merely as a rendering technique but as the
\textbf{foundational spatial capability} of Generative World Models:
systems that predict visual scene states under arbitrary conditions including
viewpoint changes, temporal dynamics, and agent interactions.

We present a unified conceptual framework organising existing methods along a
\emph{continuous spectrum}---from static multi-view reconstruction to dynamic,
interactive world generation---across three axes:
(i)~scene representation (implicit, explicit, hybrid),
(ii)~generative paradigm (per-scene optimisation, feed-forward inference,
diffusion-based synthesis),
and (iii)~world-modelling capability (geometry, dynamics, controllability).
We survey more than \textbf{160 representative methods} spanning NeRF,
3D Gaussian Splatting, generalizable reconstruction, diffusion-based NVS,
and 4-D world modeling, identify the key open challenges, and propose a
roadmap toward physically grounded, generalisable world models.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

\subsection{The Fundamental Problem}

Intelligence requires reasoning about the world beyond what is immediately
visible.
A self-driving vehicle must anticipate the scene around a blind corner;
a robot must predict how a grasped object will appear from the wrist camera;
a surgeon must mentally rotate an anatomy from a 2-D scan.
In each case the core operation is identical:
\textbf{synthesising a plausible visual state at an unobserved vantage point}.

This is the problem of \textbf{Novel View Synthesis~(\nvs)}.
Given reference images $\{\mathbf{I}_i,\boldsymbol{\pi}_i\}_{i=1}^{n}$ with
known camera poses, the goal is to render a photo-realistic image
$\hat{\mathbf{I}}$ at an arbitrary target pose $\boldsymbol{\pi}^{*}$,
faithfully preserving geometry, appearance, and lighting.

While the problem was studied in the image-based rendering
era~\citep{levoy1996light,mcmillan1995plenoptic,chen1993view,debevec1996modeling},
the past five years have witnessed an extraordinary leap:
NeRF~\citep{mildenhall2020nerf},
3DGS~\citep{kerbl2023gaussian}, and diffusion-based
methods~\citep{liu2023zero1to3,poole2023dreamfusion}
have pushed the frontier from slow per-scene reconstruction toward fast,
generalisable, and generative synthesis.

\subsection{From NVS to World Modeling}

We argue that this progression reflects a deeper conceptual shift:
\textbf{\nvs{} is becoming the spatial inference engine of Generative World
Modeling}.

\begin{quote}
  \textbf{Working Definition~(World Model).}\quad
  A \emph{Generative World Model} is a learned system that can predict the
  visual (and optionally physical) state of a scene under arbitrary
  conditions---viewpoint changes, temporal evolution, and agent
  interactions---in a manner that is \emph{geometrically consistent},
  \emph{physically plausible}, and \emph{generalisable} beyond the training
  distribution.
\end{quote}

Under this definition, \nvs{} is the special case where the only varying
condition is camera pose.
A world model must additionally handle temporal dynamics and action conditioning.
Crucially, \emph{spatial consistency enforced by \nvs{} is a prerequisite}:
a world model without geometric grounding is merely a video generator---plausible
in appearance but unreliable for spatial reasoning.

\section{Background and Unified Formulation}
\label{sec:background}

\subsection{Classical Image-Based Rendering}

Before the neural era, novel view synthesis was approached through
\emph{image-based rendering}~(IBR)~\citep{mcmillan1995plenoptic,chen1993view,levoy1996light}.
Light Field Rendering~\citep{levoy1996light} densely samples the plenoptic
function~\citep{adelson1991plenoptic} to enable view interpolation without
explicit geometry.
View-dependent texture mapping~\citep{debevec1996modeling} and
Lumigraph~\citep{gortler1996lumigraph} extended these ideas.
Structure from Motion~(SfM)~\citep{schoenberger2016sfm} and
Multi-View Stereo~(MVS)~\citep{schonberger2016mvs} provided the geometry backbone.
The key limitation of classical IBR is its dependence on dense capture:
generalisation to novel poses requires interpolation within the observed set,
not extrapolation beyond it.

\subsection{Volume Rendering and NeRF}

NeRF~\citep{mildenhall2020nerf} represents a scene as a continuous function
$F_\theta:(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{d})\mapsto(\mathbf{c},\sigma)$.
The expected colour of a ray $\mathbf{r}(s)=\mathbf{o}+s\mathbf{d}$ is:
\begin{equation}
  \hat{C}(\mathbf{r})=\int_{s_n}^{s_f} T(s)\,\sigma(\mathbf{r}(s))\,
  \mathbf{c}(\mathbf{r}(s),\mathbf{d})\,\mathrm{d}s,
  \quad T(s)=\exp\!\left(-\int_{s_n}^s\sigma(\mathbf{r}(u))\,\mathrm{d}u\right).
  \label{eq:volrender}
\end{equation}
The training objective minimises photometric reconstruction loss.
Stratified and hierarchical sampling make the continuous integral tractable.

\subsection{Gaussian Splatting}

3DGS~\citep{kerbl2023gaussian} represents a scene as $N$ anisotropic Gaussians
$\{\boldsymbol{\mu}_i,\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_i,\alpha_i,\mathbf{c}_i\}$.
Pixel colour is computed by front-to-back alpha compositing of projected Gaussians:
\begin{equation}
  \hat{C}=\sum_{i\in\mathcal{N}}\mathbf{c}_i\,\alpha_i\,\tilde{G}_i
  \prod_{j<i}(1-\alpha_j\,\tilde{G}_j),
  \label{eq:splatting}
\end{equation}
where $\tilde{G}_i$ is the 2-D projection of Gaussian~$i$.
Adaptive densification clones/splits under-reconstructed regions and
prunes redundant Gaussians.

\subsection{Unified NVS and World Modeling Formulation}

We formulate \nvs{} as a general function:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{M}_\theta:\bigl(\{\mathbf{I}_i,\boldsymbol{\pi}_i\}_{i=1}^n,\;
  \boldsymbol{\pi}^*\bigr)\longrightarrow\hat{\mathbf{I}}^*,
  \quad n\in[1,+\infty).
  \label{eq:nvs}
\end{equation}
The world-modeling extension adds time $t$ and action $\mathbf{a}$:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{M}_\theta:\bigl(\{\mathbf{I}_i,\boldsymbol{\pi}_i\}_{i=1}^n,\;
  \mathbf{a}_{0:t},\,t,\,\boldsymbol{\pi}^*\bigr)\longrightarrow\hat{\mathbf{I}}^*_t.
  \label{eq:wm}
\end{equation}
This defines five desiderata:
\textbf{D1}~geometric consistency;
\textbf{D2}~sparse/single-image generalisation;
\textbf{D3}~dynamic scene modelling;
\textbf{D4}~action conditioning;
\textbf{D5}~physical plausibility.

\section{Scene Representation Taxonomy}
\label{sec:representation}

\nvs{} methods can be classified by how they represent scene content:

\textbf{Implicit representations} encode scenes as continuous functions
(\eg, MLP, hash-grid).
Advantages: compact, resolution-independent, continuous.
Disadvantages: slow rendering, per-scene optimisation.

\textbf{Explicit representations} store scene content at discrete elements
(\eg, voxels, point clouds, Gaussians).
Advantages: fast rendering, easy editing.
Disadvantages: memory-intensive, discrete resolution limits.

\textbf{Hybrid representations} combine implicit and explicit components
(\eg, triplane + MLP, Gaussian + neural field).
Advantages: balance speed and quality.
Disadvantages: complex training pipelines.

\textbf{Structured/compositional representations} decompose scenes into
semantic entities (\eg, objects, backgrounds, agents).
Advantages: scene editing, compositional generation.
Disadvantages: require segmentation or part annotation.

\section{NeRF: Implicit Neural Radiance Fields}
\label{sec:nerf}

\subsection{Foundational NeRF Methods}

The original NeRF~\citep{mildenhall2020nerf} demonstrated that an MLP with
positional encoding could represent complex real scenes with view-dependent
appearance.
Subsequent work addressed NeRF's two principal limitations:
\emph{slow optimisation} and \emph{poor generalisation}.

\paragraph{Reducing optimisation time.}
Instant-NGP~\citep{muller2022instant} replaces the MLP backbone with a
multi-resolution hash grid, reducing training from hours to seconds
while achieving state-of-the-art quality.
TensoRF~\citep{chen2022tensorf} decomposes the radiance field into
compact tensor components (CP and VM decomposition).
Plenoxels~\citep{fridovich2022plenoxels} uses a sparse 3-D voxel grid
with spherical harmonics, achieving minute-scale optimisation without neural
networks.
DirectVoxGO~\citep{sun2022dvgo} adopts a two-stage coarse-to-fine voxel grid.
DVGO~\citep{sun2022dvgo} and TensoRF set new speed records for per-scene NeRF.

\paragraph{Improving rendering quality.}
Mip-NeRF~\citep{barron2021mipnerf} casts conical frustums instead of rays,
reducing aliasing at multiple scales.
Mip-NeRF~360~\citep{barron2022mipnerf360} extends this to unbounded 360$^\circ$
scenes via a non-linear scene contraction.
Zip-NeRF~\citep{barron2023zipnerf} combines the hash grid of Instant-NGP with
the anti-aliasing of Mip-NeRF~360.
NeRF-W~\citep{martinbrualla2021nerfw} models per-image appearance variation for
in-the-wild captures.
Ref-NeRF~\citep{verbin2022refnerf} improves specular appearance with
a physically-based reflection model.

\paragraph{Surface and geometry extraction.}
NeuS~\citep{wang2021neus} reformulates volume rendering using a signed distance
function (SDF), yielding better surface reconstruction.
VolSDF~\citep{yariv2021volsdf} provides an alternative SDF-to-density
conversion.
BakedSDF~\citep{yariv2023bakedsdf} extracts high-quality meshes from NeRF via
SDF-based optimization.

\subsection{Generalizable NeRF}

Per-scene NeRF requires lengthy per-scene optimisation, limiting scalability.
\emph{Generalisable} methods learn a shared prior across scenes, enabling
fast inference on new scenes.

pixelNeRF~\citep{yu2021pixelnerf} conditions the NeRF MLP on pixel-aligned
CNN features extracted from reference views, enabling single-image reconstruction.
IBRNet~\citep{wang2021ibrnet} uses a transformer to aggregate features from
multiple reference views at query points along the ray.
MVSNeRF~\citep{chen2021mvsnerf} builds a cost volume from stereo for
geometry-aware feature extraction.
GNT~\citep{wang2022gnt} is a fully attention-based generalizable NeRF.
SRT~\citep{sajjadi2022srt} uses a set-latent scene representation
encoded from arbitrary views.
NerfDiff~\citep{gu2023nerfdiff} augments generalizable NeRF with diffusion
priors for single-image synthesis.
ZeroRF~\citep{shi2023zerorf} achieves sparse-view 360$^\circ$ reconstruction
via per-scene optimisation with zero pretraining, leveraging factorised feature grids.

\paragraph{Few-shot and test-time methods.}
FrameNeRF~\citep{yan2024framenerf} presents a simple and efficient framework
for few-shot NVS by exploiting temporal video frames as dense pseudo views.
Recollection from Pensieve~\citep{zhou2024pensieve} learns NVS from uncalibrated
internet videos without posed supervision.
Test-Time Completion~\citep{bao2024tttcompletion} repurposes natural video
completion as a zero-shot NVS prior at test time.
Mixed Neural Voxels~\citep{wang2023mixedvoxels} enables fast multi-view video
synthesis by decomposing scenes into static and dynamic voxel grids.

\subsection{Dynamic NeRF}

Modelling non-rigid dynamic scenes is a central challenge for world modeling.

D-NeRF~\citep{pumarola2021dnerf} introduces a deformation network mapping each
spacetime point to a canonical static space.
Nerfies~\citep{park2021nerfies} and
HyperNeRF~\citep{park2021hypernerf} model complex non-rigid deformations via
latent codes and higher-dimensional ambient spaces.
Neural Scene Flow Fields~(NSFF)~\citep{li2021neural_scene_flow} models dynamic
scenes with scene flow representation.
Non-Rigid NeRF~\citep{tretschk2021nonrigid} reconstructs and synthesises
novel views of dynamic scenes from monocular video with explicit ray bending.
DyNeRF~\citep{li2022neural3d} extends NeRF to monocular video via a
time-conditioned model.
TiNeuVox~\citep{fang2022tineuvox} uses voxel features to accelerate dynamic NeRF.
K-Planes~\citep{fridovich2023kplanes} decomposes 4-D space-time into six
feature planes (three spatial, three spatial-temporal).
HexPlane~\citep{cao2023hexplane} is a concurrent work with the same idea.
NeRFPlayer~\citep{song2023nerfplayer} separately encodes static, deforming and
newly appearing regions.
Soup-of-Planes~\citep{du2023soupofplanes} enables fast view synthesis of
casual monocular videos by fitting a set of planar scenes.
Pseudo-Generalised Dynamic View~\citep{zhao2024pgdvs} synthesises dynamic views
from monocular video by combining static and dynamic video inpainting.
Decoupled Dynamic Video~\citep{wang2024decoupleddynamic} disentangles foreground
dynamics and background statics for monocular dynamic view synthesis.

\section{3D Gaussian Splatting}
\label{sec:3dgs}

\subsection{Foundational 3DGS}

3DGS~\citep{kerbl2023gaussian} achieved real-time ($>$100\,fps at 1080p)
high-quality NVS with fast training.
Its explicit, editable representation opened entirely new research directions.

\paragraph{Quality improvements.}
Mip-Splatting~\citep{yu2024mipsplatting} applies 3-D smoothing filters to
address aliasing at different viewing distances.
AbsGS~\citep{ye2024absgs} improves densification with an absolute gradient
criterion, reducing needle artifacts.
Taming 3DGS provides principled primitives for
bounded and compact reconstruction.

\paragraph{Compression and efficiency.}
LightGaussian~\citep{fan2023lightgaussian} globally prunes and vector-quantises
Gaussians, achieving $15\times$ compression with minimal quality loss.
Compact3D~\citep{lee2024compact3d} uses k-means codebooks for attribute
compression.
EAGLES~\citep{girish2024eagles} compresses Gaussians using residual vector
quantization.
Scaffold-GS~\citep{lu2023scaffoldgs} introduces anchor Gaussians that drive
neural Gaussian attribute prediction, substantially reducing storage.
HAC~\citep{chen2024hac} uses hash-grid-aided context models for extreme compression.

\paragraph{Surface reconstruction.}
SuGaR~\citep{guedon2024sugar} jointly optimises mesh and Gaussians for
high-quality surface extraction from 3DGS.
2DGS~\citep{huang2024_2dgs} uses 2-D disk Gaussians for better surface alignment.
Gaussian Opacity Fields~(GOF)~\citep{yu2024gof} combines Gaussian Splatting
with a continuous opacity field.

\paragraph{Scene editing and compositional scenes.}
GaussianEditor~\citep{chen2024gaussianeditor} performs language-guided
3-D Gaussian editing.
Gaussian Grouping~\citep{ye2023gaussian_grouping} segments 3DGS with identity
fields for part-level editing.
GALA3D~\citep{zhou2024gala3d} generates multi-object compositional scenes with
3DGS via LLM-guided layout.

\subsection{Dynamic 3DGS}

4DGS~\citep{wu2024_4dgs} extends Gaussians into 4-D space-time with a
temporal radiance field.
SpacetimeGaussians~\citep{yang2024spacetime} assigns polynomial time
trajectories to each Gaussian.
Deformable3DGS~\citep{yang2024deformable} applies a deformation network to
canonical Gaussians.
SC-GS~\citep{huang2024scgs} uses sparse control points for monocular dynamic
scenes.
Gaussian-Flow~\citep{lin2024gaussianflow} models Gaussian dynamics via
polynomial motion fields in Fourier space.
Shape-of-Motion~\citep{wang2024shapeofmotion} reconstructs per-Gaussian 3-D
motion from monocular videos.
Dynamic 3D Gaussians~\citep{luiten2024dynamic3dgs} tracks scene dynamics
through persistent Gaussian views, jointly optimising NVS and tracking.
GauFRe~\citep{liang2024gaufre} uses Gaussian deformation fields with
flow regularisation for real-time dynamic NVS.
MoVieS~\citep{sun2024movies} generates motion-aware 4-D dynamic views in
approximately one second via efficient Gaussian motion estimation.
Self-Calibrating 4D~\citep{kim2024selfcal4d} performs 4-D NVS from monocular
videos by jointly estimating camera parameters and Gaussian deformations.
Reconstruct-Inpaint-Finetune~\citep{han2024rif} addresses dynamic monocular
NVS via a three-stage pipeline of reconstruction, inpainting, and test-time
refinement.
EVA-Gaussian~\citep{hu2024evacg} achieves real-time human NVS under diverse
multi-view settings.
Articulated Gaussian~\citep{li2024articulatedgs} enables template-free
articulated Gaussian Splatting for real-time reposable dynamic view synthesis.

Splatt3R~\citep{smart2024splatt3r} builds on DUSt3R~\citep{wang2024dust3r}
for unposed image pairs.
FSGS~\citep{zhu2024fsgs} enables few-shot Gaussian Splatting via Gaussian
unpooling and monocular depth supervision.
DepthSplat~\citep{xu2024depthsplat} jointly predicts depth and Gaussians

TriplaneGaussian~\citep{zou2024triplanegaussian} combines triplane and

\section{Generalisable Feed-Forward 3D Reconstruction}
\label{sec:generalisable}

\subsection{Pose-Free and Foundation Models}

A new generation of methods aims for \emph{pose-free} generalizable reconstruction.
DUSt3R~\citep{wang2024dust3r} predicts dense pointmaps from arbitrary image pairs
without camera calibration, then aligns them via global optimisation.
MASt3R~\citep{leroy2024mast3r} adds matching capabilities to DUSt3R, enabling
accurate localisation.
MonST3R~\citep{zhang2024monst3r} extends DUSt3R to dynamic video.
VGGT~\citep{wang2025vggt} is a visual geometry grounded deep structured tokens
model for feed-forward 3-D structure prediction.

\subsection{Large Reconstruction Models (LRM)}

LRM~\citep{hong2023lrm} trains a large transformer on objaverse, enabling
single-image-to-3D in under 5 seconds.
Instant3D~\citep{li2023instant3d} generates NeRF from a single image via
a diffusion-conditioned LRM.
OpenLRM~\citep{openlrm2023} is the open-source counterpart.
LGM~\citep{tang2024lgm} learns multi-view Gaussian generation for fast
single-image 3-D.
CRM~\citep{wang2024crm} uses a convolutional reconstruction model.

\subsection{Multi-View Stereo in the Neural Era}

Classical MVS has been reinvigorated by deep learning.
MVSNet~\citep{yao2018mvsnet} introduced cost-volume-based deep MVS.
IterMVS~\citep{wang2022itermvs} and Uni-MVSNet~\citep{peng2022unimvsnet}
improved accuracy and generalisation.
These methods provide the geometry backbone for downstream NVS.

\section{Generative Paradigms for NVS}
\label{sec:generative}

\subsection{3-D-Aware GANs}

GRAF~\citep{schwarz2020graf} and pi-GAN~\citep{chan2021pigan} condition
radiance-field generators on latent codes, producing multi-view consistent images.
EG3D~\citep{chan2022efficient} introduced the triplane backbone and a
super-resolution network, dramatically improving quality and speed.
StyleNeRF~\citep{gu2022stylenerf} integrates NeRF into a StyleGAN generator.
GIRAFFE~\citep{niemeyer2021giraffe} decomposes scenes into compositional
neural feature fields.
These methods established implicit 3-D supervision via GAN training on 2-D images.

\subsection{Diffusion-Based Single-View and Sparse-View NVS}

\paragraph{Pose-conditioned diffusion.}
Zero-1-to-3~\citep{liu2023zero1to3} fine-tunes a latent diffusion model on
multi-view data, conditioning on relative pose for single-image NVS.
Zero123++~\citep{shi2023zero123pp} improves consistency across views.
One-2-3-45~\citep{liu2023one2345} lifts diffusion outputs to 3-D via MVS.
SyncDreamer~\citep{liu2023syncdreamer} jointly denoises multiple views
with shared 3-D attention.
MVDiffusion~\citep{tang2023mvdiffusion} generates multi-view consistent
images via a correspondence-aware attention mechanism.
Wonder3D~\citep{long2024wonder3d} generates cross-domain diffusion outputs
(colour + normal) for high-quality 3-D reconstruction.
EscherNet~\citep{kong2024eschernet} uses positional encoding for
arbitrary view generation.

\paragraph{Reconstruction-diffusion hybrids.}
Cat3D~\citep{gao2024cat3d} generates consistent multi-view images then
feeds them to a NeRF/3DGS pipeline.
ReconFusion~\citep{wu2024reconfusion} integrates pixel-level diffusion
priors into sparse-view NeRF.
ZeroNVS~\citep{sargent2023zeronvs} enables zero-shot NVS from a single
in-the-wild image.
Free3D~\citep{zheng2023free3d} conditions a video diffusion model on
camera trajectories for single-image novel-view sequences.
Novel View Synthesis via DDIM Inversion~\citep{xu2024ddim_nvs} repurposes
DDIM inversion of pre-trained video diffusion models as a training-free
NVS approach, leveraging the implicit geometric knowledge in video diffusion.
NVComposer~\citep{li2024nvcomposer} boosts generative NVS from multiple
sparse and unposed images by composing their contributions at the feature level.
Projected Representation Conditioning~\citep{han2024prc} proposes a
high-fidelity NVS method by conditioning the diffusion model on projected
3-D features for accurate geometric alignment.
A Single Image and Multimodality~\citep{tang2024smm} synthesises novel views
from a single image using multimodal conditioning (depth, normal, semantics).
GeoNVS~\citep{chen2024geonvs} grounds video diffusion with explicit geometric
prior for geometrically consistent NVS.
NVS-Solver~\citep{you2024nvssolver} reinterprets video diffusion as a
zero-shot NVS solver by casting view synthesis as a diffusion trajectory problem.
Novel View Extrapolation~\citep{shi2024nve} addresses the challenging case of
large-angle view extrapolation using video diffusion priors.
ViVid-1-to-3~\citep{kwak2024vivid} extends video diffusion models to
single-image NVS by aligning temporal and viewpoint consistency.
Consistent View Synthesis~\citep{tsai2023consistent} enforces pose-guided
consistency in diffusion-based NVS.
iVS-Net~\citep{chen2024ivsnet} learns human view synthesis from internet
videos without any 3-D supervision.

\subsection{Score Distillation and Text-to-3D}

DreamFusion~\citep{poole2023dreamfusion} optimises a NeRF to maximise
the log-likelihood under a pre-trained 2-D diffusion model.
Magic3D~\citep{lin2023magic3d} uses coarse-to-fine optimisation with
mesh refinement.
ProlificDreamer~\citep{wang2023prolificdreamer} introduces variational
score distillation for higher diversity and fidelity.
Fantasia3D~\citep{chen2023fantasia3d} disentangles geometry and appearance
for editable results.
GaussianDreamer~\citep{yi2024gaussiandreamer} uses SDS to initialise and
optimise 3DGS primitives.
Lucid Dreamer~\citep{chung2023luciddreamer} improves SDS via interval score
matching.

\subsection{Multi-View Diffusion Models}

MVDream~\citep{shi2023mvdream} trains a multi-view diffusion model on
3-D-consistent data for 3-D-aware generation.
SyncDreamer~\citep{liu2023syncdreamer} uses feature synchronisation across views.
Wonder3D~\citep{long2024wonder3d} generates colour and normal maps jointly.
Consistent View Synthesis~\citep{tsai2023consistent} enforces long-range
temporal and viewpoint consistency via diffusion.
GeNVS~\citep{chan2023genvs} conditions a diffusion model on a NeRF scene
representation.

\section{Towards Generative World Modeling}
\label{sec:world}

\subsection{Dynamic Scenes and 4-D World Representation}

The key step from \nvs{} to world modeling requires modelling scene dynamics.
Beyond the dynamic NeRF and 4DGS methods surveyed above, recent work targets
\emph{generalizable} 4-D scene generation.

ViewCrafter~\citep{yu2024viewcrafter} conditions a video diffusion model on
camera trajectories, producing temporally coherent novel-view sequences.
4DiM~\citep{watson2024_4dim} generates 4-D scenes from sparse images via
a 4-D diffusion model.
SV4D~\citep{xie2024sv4d} extends stable video diffusion to 4-D object generation.
SV3D~\citep{voleti2024sv3d} generates novel multi-view images and 3-D content
from a single image using latent video diffusion, achieving high multi-view
consistency via orbital camera conditioning.
CAT4D~\citep{wu2024cat4d} extends multi-view diffusion to 4-D creation,
jointly modelling viewpoint and temporal dimensions.
Consistent4D~\citep{jiang2024consistent4d} produces temporally consistent
4-D content via a cascade diffusion approach.
DreamGaussian4D~\citep{ren2023dreamgaussian4d} combines SDS with 4DGS for
text-driven 4-D generation.
Diffusion Priors for Dynamic NVS~\citep{li2024diffpriordynamic} leverages
video diffusion priors to regularise monocular dynamic view synthesis.
Dynamic View from Small Motion~\citep{li2024smallmotion} specialises in
scenes captured with small camera motions, common in handheld videos.

\subsection{Neural Scene Simulators for Autonomous Driving}

Autonomous driving is a prime application of NVS-as-world-model.

UniSim~\citep{yang2023unisim} constructs a closed-loop neural sensor simulator
for urban driving, rendering novel views with controllable scene edits.
MARS~\citep{wu2023mars} composes NeRF-based backgrounds and foreground actors
for urban scene simulation.
OmniRe~\citep{chen2024omnire} builds an all-in-one neural driving simulation
framework.
EmerNeRF~\citep{yang2023emernerf} is a self-supervised approach for emergent
dynamic modelling in driving scenes.
Street Gaussians~\citep{yan2024streetgaussian} applies 3DGS to urban driving.
PVG~\citep{chen2023pvg} uses periodic vibration Gaussians for real-time
driving scene reconstruction.
StreetCrafter~\citep{yan2024streetcrafter} extends video diffusion models with
controllable camera motion for street-view synthesis.
Sat2Vid~\citep{wu2022sat2vid} generates street-view panoramic video from a
single satellite image, bridging aerial and ground-level views.
Wild-GS~\citep{xu2024wildgs} achieves real-time NVS from unconstrained
in-the-wild photo collections via appearance decoupling in 3DGS.
InSpatio-WorldFM~\citep{chen2025inspatioworld} is an open-source real-time
generative frame model for spatial world understanding.

\subsection{Generative World Models and Foundation Models}

Large-scale world foundation models represent the apex of this trajectory.

GAIA-1~\citep{hu2023gaia} casts world modeling as unsupervised sequence prediction
on video, text, and action tokens for autonomous driving.
Genie~\citep{bruce2024genie} learns to generate interactive 2-D game worlds
from unsupervised video.
Genie~2~\citep{parkerholder2024genie2} generates persistent 3-D environments
from a single image and supports interactive first-person navigation.
Cosmos~\citep{agarwal2025cosmos} trains world foundation models on internet-scale
video for physical world simulation.
Pandora~\citep{xiang2024pandora} interleaves language and video tokens for
interactive world generation.
WonderJourney~\citep{yu2023wonderjourney} generates perpetual scene explorations
via text-guided chains of image generation.
WonderWorld~\citep{yu2024wonderworld} generates interactive 3-D scenes with

\subsection{Human Novel View Synthesis}

Synthesising novel views of humans is a critical subproblem with applications

\paragraph{Neural implicit human models.}

Rotationally-Temporally Consistent~\citep{liu2021rtcnvs} enforces both
rotation and temporal consistency for human performance video synthesis.
NHR~\citep{wu2020nhr} adopts multi-view point-cloud-based rendering for
dynamic human synthesis.

CFSynthesis~\citep{li2023cfsynth} enables controllable and free-view 3-D human
video synthesis via a disentangled representation.
iVS-Net~\citep{chen2024ivsnet} learns human view synthesis from internet
videos without any 3-D supervision.
Blur2Sharp~\citep{dong2024blur2sharp} addresses human novel pose and view
synthesis from blurry input with a generative prior.
Diffuman4D~\citep{zheng2024diffuman4d} achieves 4-D consistent human view
synthesis from sparse-view videos via video diffusion.
EVA-Gaussian~\citep{hu2024evacg} achieves real-time 3DGS-based human NVS
under diverse multi-view settings.

\section{Datasets and Benchmarks}

\subsection{Object-Level Datasets}

\textbf{ShapeNet}~\citep{chang2015shapenet}: 51K 3-D CAD models; foundation for
single-image reconstruction training.

\subsection{Video and World Modelling Metrics}

\textbf{FVD}~\citep{unterthiner2019fvd} (Fréchet Video Distance): distributional
quality of generated video sequences.
\textbf{FID}~\citep{heusel2017fid}: Fréchet Inception Distance for image quality.
\textbf{Temporal consistency}: average cosine similarity of DINO features
between consecutive frames.
\textbf{Task-specific}: for driving, closed-loop collision rate, route completion,
imitation gap; for robotics, task success rate.

\subsection{Limitations of Current Evaluation}

PSNR/SSIM penalise perceptually plausible but pixel-misaligned generations,
especially under large viewpoint changes.
FVD captures perceptual quality but not geometric accuracy.
No existing benchmark jointly evaluates geometry, dynamics, action
responsiveness, and physical plausibility.
This evaluation gap is itself a major open problem.

\paragraph{Towards systematic evaluation.}
The work on Systematic Evaluation of NVS~\citep{chen2024sysnvs} provides
a principled analysis of how existing benchmarks differ and proposes
controlled evaluation protocols separating appearance and geometry quality.
Non-Aligned Reference IQA~\citep{zhang2024nariqanvs} addresses the fundamental
problem of quality assessment when reference and synthesised views are not
pixel-aligned, proposing a learned non-aligned metric for NVS.
VGGT-X~\citep{liu2025vggtx} extends VGGT to dense NVS, providing strong
baselines for the emerging class of feed-forward 3-D foundation models.

\section{Open Problems and Research Roadmap}
\label{sec:open}

\subsection{The Geometry--Generation Trade-Off}

Reconstruction methods deliver geometric precision but lack generative priors;
diffusion methods provide rich priors but can violate multi-view consistency.
Methods like ReconFusion, Cat3D, and ViewCrafter begin to bridge this gap,
but the fundamental tension remains.
Principled solutions (\eg, geometry-grounded diffusion, diffusion-seeded 3DGS)
are an active frontier.

\subsection{Long-Horizon Temporal Coherence}

Existing 4-D methods maintain coherence over a few seconds.
Two promising directions:
(1) \emph{Persistent scene memory}: Gaussian maps that accumulate observations
across time and preserve global consistency;
(2) \emph{World model pre-training}: leveraging internet-scale video prediction
as a temporal prior that can be fine-tuned to specific domains.

\subsection{Physical Grounding}

Current methods learn appearance correlations, not physical laws.
Encouraging directions include:
PAC-NeRF~\citep{li2023pac} for continuum mechanics integration;
physics-informed deformable Gaussians for material simulation.
The central challenge is integrating differentiable physics into the rendering pipeline
in a way that scales to complex, open-world scenes.

\subsection{Open-Vocabulary Semantic Integration}

Methods like LERF~\citep{kerr2023lerf} ground CLIP features in 3-D via NeRF,
enabling open-vocabulary 3-D queries.
LangSplat~\citep{qin2024langsplat} brings language grounding to 3DGS.
Feature3DGS~\citep{zhou2024feature3dgs} distils various 2-D foundation model features.
Extending these to dynamic, actionable scenes is a key step toward language-grounded
world models.

\subsection{Evaluation and Benchmarks}

We propose a five-axis world-modelling benchmark:
(1)~3-D consistency under adversarial viewpoints;
(2)~temporal coherence over long horizons ($>$30 s);
(3)~action responsiveness in interactive settings;
(4)~physical plausibility via physics-engine verification;
(5)~open-world generalisation from a single image.

\subsection{Research Roadmap}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,label=\textbf{R\arabic*.}]
  \item Unified architectures combining explicit 3-D with diffusion priors.
  \item Internet-scale feed-forward generalisation for open-world NVS.
  \item Differentiable physics priors in 4-D world models.
  \item Language and semantic grounding for action-conditioned generation.
  \item Comprehensive world-modelling evaluation suite.
\end{enumerate}

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have surveyed Novel View Synthesis through the lens of Generative World Modeling,
tracing the evolution from classical image-based rendering through NeRF,
3D Gaussian Splatting, feed-forward reconstruction, and diffusion-based synthesis
toward 4-D world generation.
Our unified continuous-spectrum taxonomy organises more than 160 methods across
three axes (input regime, temporal scope, controllability) and makes explicit
their world-modelling implications.

The field is at an inflection point.
Spatial grounding from NVS and temporal/action capabilities from world modeling
are actively converging, as evidenced by systems such as Genie~2, Cosmos,
ViewCrafter, and the neural driving simulators.
Closing the geometry--generation trade-off, achieving long-horizon coherence,
and grounding predictions in physical laws are the primary challenges ahead.
